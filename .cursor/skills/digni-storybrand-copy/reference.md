# StoryBrand × Digni — reference worksheet

Use this to align drafts before editing translation files. Not for publication.

## BrandScript-style prompts

**Hero (customer)**

- Who is the specific audience segment?
- What do they want that Digni can help with (one clear desire)?

**Problems**

- External: What practical thing is broken or slow?
- Internal: How does that make them feel?
- Philosophical: Why is it just plain wrong that they stay stuck?

**Guide (Digni)**

- Empathy: In one line, “We get it because…”
- Authority: Proof (results, logos, years, methodology)—compact, credible.

**Plan**

- Process: 3–4 numbered steps from “first contact” to “value delivered.”
- Agreement: What you promise (communication, timelines, risk reversal).

**CTAs**

- Direct: The main commercial action.
- Transitional: Low-risk next step (resource, diagnostic, newsletter).

**Stakes**

- Failure: What worsens if they delay or choose noise over clarity?
- Success: What specific win they see/feel/measure after engaging?

## One-liner scaffold

Template (replace brackets):

> Every [hero type] wants to [want]. The problem is [external problem], which leaves them feeling [internal]. We help them [plan in plain language] so they can [success] without [failure risk].

## Page-level checklist

- [ ] Hero answers grunt test (what / better life / what to do).
- [ ] Customer is hero; Digni is guide with proof.
- [ ] Problem ladder present (external → internal → philosophical).
- [ ] Clear process plan near consideration point.
- [ ] Direct + transitional CTAs where appropriate.
- [ ] Success and failure stakes visible (not fear-mongering).
- [ ] Growth operator: outcome, mechanism, proof, risk removal before primary CTA.
- [ ] Locales updated if applicable.
